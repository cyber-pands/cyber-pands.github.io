About Me

I am a cybersecurity professional who believes real understanding comes from building systems, observing how they fail, and documenting what those failures teach us. I enjoy working close to the data, asking why something happened, and turning that understanding into practical security improvements.

How I Approach Cybersecurity

I do not see cybersecurity as a checklist of tools or certifications. My approach is rooted in understanding context. I care more about why an event occurred than simply acknowledging that an alert was raised.

Whether I am looking at authentication logs, web traffic, or suspicious system activity, I prefer to break problems down step by step. I start from raw data, examine patterns and behavior, and work toward conclusions that actually make sense in a real environment.
